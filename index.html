About

I'm a policy advisor, researcher, and writer whose career has taken me from a remote indigenous village in Panama to the halls of the United Nations General Assembly. I specialize in human displacement, immigration policy, democracy, and foreign assistance — and I believe that the best policy writing doesn't just inform, it compels.

01

On the Ground

Two years as a Peace Corps Volunteer in Panama's Comarca Ngäbe-Buglé, teaching English and expanding access to HIV treatment alongside Panama's Ministry of Health. Saw firsthand how policy can perpetuate — or dismantle — inequality.

02

In the Room

Served as a U.S. delegate to the UN General Assembly. Negotiated resolutions on economic development and migration. Drafted speeches and briefing materials for senior leadership at the State Department's Bureau of Democracy, Human Rights, and Labor.

03

On the Page

Published in the Yale Journal of International Affairs. Writing Crossing Points, a Substack on immigration, detention, and the border between policy and lived experience. Turning years of fieldwork and research into long-form narrative.

“I believe on-the-ground experience is crucial for policymakers. You can’t write about displacement from a distance.”
Liam Comer-Weaver
